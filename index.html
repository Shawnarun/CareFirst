CHANNELING

Channeling in a hospital refers to the process of scheduling an appointment with a specific healthcare provider or specialist through a centralized system.

PHARMACY

Pharmacy in hospital is a specialized medical department that manages the preparation and dispensation of medications and provides pharmaceutical care to patients admitted in the hospital

OPD

OPD (Outpatient Department) in a hospital is a unit where patients receive medical diagnosis, treatment, and consultation without being admitted to the hospital.

LABORATORY

A laboratory in a hospital is a facility that conducts diagnostic tests on patient samples to aid in the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of diseases.

PHYSIOTHERAPY

Physiotherapy in hospital is a medical practice that utilizes exercise, manual therapy, and specialized equipment to promote healing, manage pain, and improve mobility and function for patients with physical impairments or disabilities.

MINOR SURGERY

Minor surgery refers to surgical procedures that are minimally invasive and generally require only local anesthesia, and are usually performed in a hospital setting.

WOUND CARE

Wound care in hospital refers to the process of treating and managing wounds to promote healing and prevent infection.

DENTAL CARE

Dental care in a hospital setting involves the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of oral diseases and conditions by qualified dental professionals.

ECHO & ECG

ECHO (echocardiogram) is an ultrasound test that creates images of the heart, while ECG (electrocardiogram) is a test that records the electrical activity of the heart to detect abnormalities.
